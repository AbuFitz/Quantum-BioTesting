03 Before Your Test

Accurate results
require
careful preparation.

To ensure every biomarker is measured at its true baseline, please follow these preparation guidelines in the 12–24 hours before your appointment.

Please do

  • ✓ Fast for a minimum of 10–12 hours before your appointment (water is permitted and encouraged)
  • ✓ Stay well hydrated — drink at least 500ml of water in the hour before your visit
  • ✓ Continue taking all prescribed medications as normal unless your GP advises otherwise
  • ✓ Get a full night's sleep before — cortisol and hormonal readings are sensitive to fatigue
  • ✓ Wear comfortable, loose-fitting clothing with easy access to your arm
  • ✓ Arrive 5 minutes early and relax — a calm resting state gives the most reliable readings
Timing note: Morning appointments (before 10am) are recommended for the most consistent hormonal baseline measurements.

Please avoid

  • ✕ Eating or drinking anything other than water in the 12-hour fasting window
  • ✕ Strenuous exercise for 24 hours prior — this elevates CK, CRP, and can skew full blood count
  • ✕ Alcohol for a minimum of 48 hours before — alcohol affects liver enzymes, lipids, and glucose
  • ✕ New supplements — do not start any new vitamins or supplements within 72 hours of testing
  • ✕ Smoking or vaping on the morning of your test — affects haematological markers
  • ✕ For female hormone testing — avoid testing in the first 3 days of your menstrual cycle where possible
Important: If you are taking any blood-thinning medication, please inform the clinic at booking. We will advise accordingly.
