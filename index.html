How to Prepare

Simple steps for
accurate results.

Following these guidelines before your appointment ensures the most accurate and clinically meaningful results from your test.

01

Fast for 8–12 Hours

Please fast for a minimum of 8 hours and ideally 10–12 hours before your appointment. Water is permitted and encouraged. A morning appointment with an overnight fast is the simplest approach for most people.

02

Stay Well Hydrated

Drink plenty of water in the 24 hours before your appointment and on the morning of your test. Good hydration makes veins easier to access, makes the blood draw more comfortable, and improves the accuracy of certain markers in your results.

03

Continue All Medications

Continue taking all prescribed medications as normal unless your doctor has specifically advised otherwise. Where possible, take vitamin and mineral supplements after your blood draw rather than before, to avoid influencing certain markers.

04

Avoid Strenuous Exercise

Avoid intense physical exercise in the 24 hours before your appointment. Heavy exercise can temporarily elevate inflammatory markers, creatine kinase, and certain enzyme levels, which may affect the clinical interpretation of your results.

Have specific medical requirements? If you take regular medication, have a health condition, or are unsure about any aspect of preparation, please contact our team before your appointment. We will advise you personally to ensure the most accurate results possible.
