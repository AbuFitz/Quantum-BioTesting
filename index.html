05 Before Your Test

Accurate results require
careful preparation.

To ensure every biomarker reflects your true physiological baseline, please follow these guidelines in the 12–24 hours before your appointment.

Please do

  • ✓ Fast for a minimum of 10–12 hours before your appointment. Water is encouraged throughout.
  • ✓ Drink at least 500ml of water in the hour before your visit. Hydration significantly improves sample quality.
  • ✓ Continue all prescribed medications as normal unless your GP has advised otherwise.
  • ✓ Aim for a full night's sleep the night before — cortisol and hormonal markers are sensitive to fatigue.
  • ✓ Wear loose, comfortable clothing with easy access to your upper arm.
  • ✓ Book a morning appointment where possible — before 10am gives the most consistent hormonal readings.
Note: If you are taking anticoagulant medication, please inform the clinic team at the time of booking so appropriate preparation guidance can be provided.

Please avoid

  • ✕ Eating or drinking anything other than water during the 12-hour fasting window.
  • ✕ Strenuous exercise for at least 24 hours before — this elevates CK, CRP, and can skew haematological results.
  • ✕ Alcohol for a minimum of 48 hours prior to testing — it affects liver enzymes, lipids, and glucose readings.
  • ✕ New supplements — do not start any new vitamins or nutritional supplements within 72 hours of your test.
  • ✕ Smoking or vaping on the morning of your appointment — this affects multiple haematological markers.
  • ✕ Hormone testing during days 1–3 of your menstrual cycle — results during this window may not reflect your typical baseline.
Important: In the event of a medical emergency, contact 999 or attend your nearest Accident & Emergency department immediately. This is not an emergency service.
