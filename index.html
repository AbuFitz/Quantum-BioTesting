Before Your Test

Accurate results require careful preparation.

To ensure every biomarker reflects your true physiological baseline, please follow these guidelines in the 12–48 hours before your appointment.

Please do

  • ✓ Fast for a minimum of 10–12 hours before your appointment. Water is actively encouraged throughout the fasting window.
  • ✓ Drink at least 500ml of water in the hour before your visit. Adequate hydration significantly improves sample quality and ease of collection.
  • ✓ Continue all prescribed medications as normal unless your GP has specifically advised otherwise in relation to this test.
  • ✓ Aim for a full night's sleep beforehand. Cortisol and hormonal markers are particularly sensitive to fatigue and disrupted sleep.
  • ✓ Wear loose, comfortable clothing with easy access to the upper arm for the blood draw.
  • ✓ Book a morning slot before 10am where possible — this produces the most consistent hormonal and cortisol baseline readings.
Note: If you are taking anticoagulant medication such as warfarin, please inform the clinic team when booking so that appropriate guidance can be provided.

Please avoid

  • ✕ Eating or drinking anything other than water during the 12-hour fasting period before your test.
  • ✕ Strenuous exercise for at least 24 hours before your appointment — this elevates CK, CRP, white cell counts, and can significantly skew haematological results.
  • ✕ Alcohol for a minimum of 48 hours prior — alcohol directly affects liver enzyme readings, lipid levels, and glucose metabolism.
  • ✕ Starting any new supplements or vitamins within 72 hours of your test — this can artificially elevate certain biomarker levels.
  • ✕ Smoking or vaping on the morning of your appointment — this affects multiple haematological and cardiovascular markers.
  • ✕ Booking hormone testing during days 1–3 of the menstrual cycle — results during this window will not reflect a reliable hormonal baseline.
Important: This is not an emergency service. In the event of a medical emergency, contact 999 or attend your nearest Accident & Emergency department immediately.
