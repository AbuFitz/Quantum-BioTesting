Before Your Appointment

What to expect & how to prepare.

Following these simple steps ensures the most accurate results and the most comfortable appointment experience possible.

Fasting Required — 8 to 12 Hours

You must fast for 8 to 12 hours before your appointment. This means no food or drink (water is fine and encouraged) during this period. Fasting is essential for the accuracy of key metabolic, lipid, and blood glucose markers. Morning appointment slots work well for most people.

Be Well Hydrated on the Day

Drink plenty of water on the morning of your appointment and in the 24 hours before. Good hydration makes veins easier to access, making the blood draw quicker and more comfortable. It also ensures the accuracy of certain markers in your results.

Medications & Supplements

Continue taking all prescribed medications as normal unless your doctor has specifically advised otherwise. Where possible, take vitamin or mineral supplements after your blood draw rather than before, to avoid influencing the readings of relevant markers.

Avoid Strenuous Exercise Beforehand

Avoid intense or strenuous physical exercise in the 24 hours before your appointment. Heavy exercise can temporarily elevate inflammatory markers and certain enzyme levels, which may affect the interpretation of your results.

Important: If you have specific medical conditions, take regular medication, or are unsure about any aspect of preparation, please contact our team before your appointment. We will advise you directly to ensure the most accurate results possible.
