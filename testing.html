About the Premium BioTest

Why standard blood tests don't tell the full story

A routine NHS blood test typically measures between 10 and 20 markers. Useful for identifying acute problems, but insufficient for detecting the subtle imbalances that precede chronic disease.

The Quantum Premium BioTest measures over 300 biomarkers — providing a granular, evidence-based picture of how every major body system is performing. This includes markers not routinely tested on the NHS, such as advanced lipid sub-fractions, full hormone panels, micronutrient levels, and early inflammatory indicators.

All samples are processed in UKAS-accredited, ISO 15189-certified laboratories. High-sensitivity techniques including LC-MS/MS mass spectrometry are used where clinically appropriate to ensure maximum accuracy.

Clinical note

Reference ranges explained

Your report shows results alongside laboratory reference ranges. A result outside the reference range does not necessarily indicate disease — your GP consultation will contextualise all findings against your individual clinical picture.

Included in every package

GP consultation

Every Premium BioTest includes a 30-minute consultation with a GMC-registered GP to review your results and discuss any findings that need further investigation or clinical attention.

Full biomarker list

Complete Premium BioTest panel

All markers listed below are included in the standard Premium BioTest package. No add-ons required.

11 biomarkers

Hormones

A comprehensive hormone panel covering the key axes of endocrine function. Particularly valuable for individuals experiencing fatigue, low mood, weight changes, fertility concerns, or symptoms of hormonal imbalance.

Testosterone (Total) Testosterone (Free) SHBG Oestradiol (E2) Progesterone TSH Free T4 Free T3 DHEA-S Cortisol (AM) IGF-1
11 biomarkers

Vitamins & Minerals

Micronutrient deficiencies are significantly underdiagnosed and are associated with fatigue, immune dysfunction, bone health, cognitive decline, and cardiovascular risk. This panel identifies deficiencies and borderline levels.

Vitamin D (25-OH) Vitamin B12 (Active) Folate (Serum) Ferritin Serum Iron Transferrin Saturation TIBC Zinc Magnesium (RBC) Selenium Copper
7 biomarkers

Inflammation & Immune Function

Chronic low-grade inflammation is now recognised as a major driver of cardiovascular disease, diabetes, and metabolic syndrome. This panel detects systemic inflammatory load using a combination of acute-phase proteins and immune cell markers.

hsCRP (High-Sensitivity) ESR Fibrinogen Homocysteine WBC Differential Neutrophil:Lymphocyte Ratio IL-6
8 biomarkers

Liver Function

A full liver function panel assessing hepatic inflammation, synthetic function, and biliary obstruction. Elevated markers may indicate alcohol-related liver injury, non-alcoholic fatty liver disease (NAFLD), or other hepatic conditions.

ALT (Alanine Aminotransferase) AST (Aspartate Aminotransferase) GGT ALP (Alkaline Phosphatase) Bilirubin (Total) Albumin Total Protein Globulin
7 biomarkers

Kidney Function

Kidney disease is often asymptomatic until significant function has been lost. This panel provides early detection of renal impairment through estimated GFR and cystatin C — a more sensitive marker of early kidney disease than creatinine alone.

eGFR (estimated GFR) Creatinine Cystatin C Urea Uric Acid Sodium Potassium
9 biomarkers

Cardiovascular Risk

A comprehensive cardiovascular panel including advanced lipid fractions beyond standard cholesterol testing. Lp(a), ApoB, and ApoA1 are increasingly recognised as superior predictors of atherosclerotic cardiovascular disease (ASCVD) risk compared to total cholesterol alone.

Total Cholesterol HDL Cholesterol LDL Cholesterol Triglycerides Lipoprotein(a) — Lp(a) ApoB ApoA1 Non-HDL Cholesterol TC:HDL Ratio
7 biomarkers

Full Blood Count

A full differential blood count evaluating red blood cells, white blood cells, and platelets. Provides information on anaemia, infection, immune status, and haematological conditions. A key baseline for any comprehensive health assessment.

Haemoglobin WBC (White Blood Cell Count) WBC Differential Platelets Haematocrit MCV MCHC
5 biomarkers

Diabetes & Metabolic Health

Pre-diabetes and insulin resistance can be present for years before clinical diagnosis. This panel assesses both short and long-term glycaemic control, together with HOMA-IR — an indirect measure of insulin resistance — for earlier identification of metabolic dysfunction.

HbA1c (Glycated Haemoglobin) Fasting Glucose Fasting Insulin HOMA-IR C-Peptide
5 biomarkers

Bone Health

Bone density loss begins silently, often long before it becomes symptomatic. This panel evaluates key markers of bone metabolism and calcium homeostasis, including parathyroid hormone (PTH), which regulates calcium and phosphate balance.

Calcium (Adjusted) Parathyroid Hormone (PTH) Phosphate ALP (Bone Isoform) Vitamin K2 (MK-7)
Laboratory standards

NHS-grade analytical methods

All Quantum BioTest samples are processed using the same analytical platforms and quality standards as the NHS. Our partner laboratories hold UKAS accreditation to ISO 15189 — the international standard for medical testing laboratories.

✓

LC-MS/MS mass spectrometry

Used for hormone and vitamin panels where highest analytical sensitivity and specificity is required. Gold-standard method for steroid hormones and Vitamin D.

✓

High-sensitivity immunoassay

Applied to inflammatory markers including hsCRP, where sub-clinical inflammation must be detected at concentrations below 1 mg/L.

✓

External quality assurance (EQA)

Our laboratories participate in national and international EQA schemes to ensure ongoing comparability and accuracy against global benchmarks.

✓

Clinical scientist review

All results are reviewed by a qualified biomedical scientist before being authorised. Abnormal findings are flagged for priority GP review.
