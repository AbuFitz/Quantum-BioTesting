2. Data we collect

We collect and process the following categories of personal data:

Identity & Contact Data: Full name, date of birth, email address, telephone number, and residential address.

Appointment Data: Preferred clinic location, appointment date and time, and correspondence relating to your booking.

Special Category Health Data: As a medical service, we necessarily process health data including biological sex, current medications, medical history, and all laboratory test results. This data is classified as special category data under UK GDPR Article 9 and receives the highest level of protection.

Technical Data: IP address, browser type, operating system, and anonymised website usage data (analytics cookies, subject to your consent).

Communications Data: Records of any correspondence with us by email, telephone, or web form.

4. Data sharing & third parties

We never sell your personal data. We may share your data only with:

  • Accredited laboratories: Your biological samples and booking details are shared with our UKAS ISO 15189 accredited laboratory partners solely for the purpose of conducting your tests.
  • GMC-registered physicians: Your test results and relevant health information are shared with the physician conducting your consultation.
  • Clinic partners: Limited booking details (name, appointment time) are shared with the CQC-registered clinic at which your appointment takes place.
  • IT and hosting providers: Our data infrastructure providers are bound by data processing agreements and may not use your data for any other purpose.
  • Regulatory authorities: We may be required to disclose data to the CQC, MHRA, ICO, or other statutory bodies if legally obligated.

All third parties are subject to contractual obligations consistent with UK GDPR requirements.

5. Data retention

We retain your data for as long as necessary for the purpose for which it was collected, subject to the following minimum periods:

  • Clinical health records (test results, physician notes): 8 years from the date of your test, in accordance with NHS/MHRA guidance for private medical records.
  • Booking and financial records: 7 years for HMRC compliance purposes.
  • Marketing consent records: Until you withdraw consent, or 3 years from last engagement.
  • Website analytics data: Up to 26 months in anonymised form.

After the retention period expires, data is securely and permanently deleted or anonymised.

6. Your rights under UK GDPR

You have the following rights regarding your personal data:

  • Right to access: Request a copy of the personal data we hold about you (Subject Access Request).
  • Right to rectification: Ask us to correct inaccurate or incomplete data.
  • Right to erasure: Ask us to delete your personal data, subject to our legal obligations to retain clinical records.
  • Right to restriction: Ask us to pause processing of your data in certain circumstances.
  • Right to data portability: Receive your data in a structured, commonly used format.
  • Right to object: Object to processing based on our legitimate interests.
  • Right to withdraw consent: Where processing is based on consent, you may withdraw it at any time without affecting the lawfulness of prior processing.

To exercise any of these rights, contact us at privacy@quantumbiotesting.co.uk. We will respond within 30 days.

7. Security

We implement appropriate technical and organisational measures to protect your personal data against unauthorised access, accidental loss, destruction, or alteration. These measures include:

  • Encryption of data at rest and in transit (TLS 1.2+)
  • Role-based access controls limiting staff access to personal data
  • Regular security assessments and penetration testing
  • Staff training on data protection and information security
  • Incident response procedures in compliance with ICO notification requirements

In the event of a personal data breach that poses a risk to your rights and freedoms, we will notify the ICO within 72 hours and inform you directly where required.

8. Cookies & tracking

We use cookies and similar technologies on our website. Cookies are small text files stored on your device.

Essential cookies (always active): Required for the site to function, including session management and security. These cannot be disabled.

Analytics cookies (consent required): We use anonymised analytics to understand how visitors interact with our site. No personally identifiable information is collected through analytics.

You can manage your cookie preferences at any time using the consent banner, or by clearing your browser cookies.

9. International transfers

Your personal data is processed and stored in the United Kingdom. We do not transfer your data outside the UK or EEA without ensuring adequate protections are in place. Where any transfer does occur, it is protected by standard contractual clauses or equivalent safeguards approved by the ICO.

10. Children

Our services are available only to persons aged 18 and over. We do not knowingly collect personal data from individuals under 18. If you believe we have collected data from a minor, please contact us immediately at privacy@quantumbiotesting.co.uk.

11. Changes to this policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time. Material changes will be notified by email (where we hold your address) and by a prominent notice on this page for at least 30 days before taking effect. The date at the top of this page always reflects the most recent revision.

12. How to complain

If you are unhappy with how we have handled your personal data, please contact us in the first instance at privacy@quantumbiotesting.co.uk.

If you remain dissatisfied, you have the right to lodge a complaint with the Information Commissioner's Office (ICO):
