2. Data we collect

We collect and process the following categories of personal data:

Identity & Contact Data: Full name, date of birth, email address, telephone number, and residential address.

Appointment Data: Preferred clinic location, appointment date and time, and booking correspondence.

Special Category Health Data: As a medical service, we necessarily process health data including biological sex, current medications, medical history, and all laboratory test results. This is classified as special category data under UK GDPR Article 9 and receives the highest level of protection.

Technical Data: IP address, browser type, operating system, and anonymised website usage data (analytics cookies, subject to your consent).

Communications Data: Records of any correspondence with us by email, telephone, or web form.

4. Data sharing & third parties

We never sell your personal data. We may share your data only with:

  • Accredited laboratories: Your biological samples and booking details are shared with our UKAS ISO 15189 accredited laboratory partners solely for conducting your tests.
  • GMC-registered physicians: Your test results and relevant health information are shared with the physician conducting any included consultation.
  • Clinic partners: Limited booking details (name, appointment time) are shared with the CQC-registered clinic at which your appointment takes place.
  • IT and hosting providers: Bound by data processing agreements and may not use your data for any other purpose.
  • Regulatory authorities: We may be required to disclose data to the CQC, MHRA, ICO, or other statutory bodies if legally obligated.

5. Data retention

We retain your data for as long as necessary for the purpose for which it was collected:

  • Clinical health records (test results, physician notes): 8 years from your test date, per NHS/MHRA guidance for private medical records.
  • Booking and financial records: 7 years for HMRC compliance.
  • Marketing consent records: Until you withdraw consent, or 3 years from last engagement.
  • Website analytics data: Up to 26 months in anonymised form.

After the retention period expires, data is securely and permanently deleted or anonymised.

6. Your rights under UK GDPR

You have the following rights regarding your personal data:

  • Right to access: Request a copy of the personal data we hold about you.
  • Right to rectification: Ask us to correct inaccurate or incomplete data.
  • Right to erasure: Ask us to delete your personal data, subject to legal obligations to retain clinical records.
  • Right to restriction: Ask us to pause processing of your data in certain circumstances.
  • Right to data portability: Receive your data in a structured, commonly used format.
  • Right to object: Object to processing based on our legitimate interests.
  • Right to withdraw consent: Where processing is based on consent, you may withdraw it at any time.

To exercise any right, contact us at hello@quantumbt.co.uk. We will respond within 30 days.

7. Security

We implement appropriate technical and organisational measures to protect your personal data, including:

  • Encryption of data at rest and in transit (TLS 1.2+)
  • Role-based access controls limiting staff access to personal data
  • Regular security assessments
  • Staff training on data protection
  • Incident response procedures in compliance with ICO notification requirements

8. Cookies & tracking

Essential cookies (always active): Required for the site to function. These cannot be disabled.

Analytics cookies (consent required): We use anonymised analytics to understand how visitors interact with our site. No personally identifiable information is collected through analytics.

You can manage your cookie preferences at any time using the consent banner on our site.

9. International transfers

Your personal data is processed and stored in the United Kingdom. We do not transfer your data outside the UK or EEA without ensuring adequate protections are in place.

10. Children

Our services are available only to persons aged 18 and over. We do not knowingly collect personal data from individuals under 18. If you believe we have collected data from a minor, please contact us immediately.

11. Changes to this policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time. Material changes will be notified by email (where we hold your address) and by a prominent notice on this page for at least 30 days before taking effect.
