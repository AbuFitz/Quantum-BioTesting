Privacy Policy

Last updated: January 2026. This Privacy Policy explains how Quantum BioTesting Ltd ("we", "us", "our") collects and processes your personal data when you use our website or services. We are the data controller and are registered with the Information Commissioner's Office (ICO) in England.

1. Who we are

Quantum BioTesting Ltd is a private health diagnostics company registered in England and Wales (Company No. 12345678). Our registered address is 1 Harley Street, London W1G 9QD. We are registered with the Information Commissioner's Office under registration number ZXXXXXXX.

For data protection enquiries, contact our Data Protection Officer at: dpo@quantumbiotesting.co.uk

2. Data we collect

Personal identification data

  • Full name and date of birth
  • Email address and telephone number
  • Billing and correspondence address
  • Photo ID (verified at clinic visit)

Health and medical data (Special Category Data)

  • Blood test results and biomarker values
  • Declared medications, supplements, and medical history (if provided)
  • Clinical notes from GP consultation
  • Personalised health report content

Technical and usage data

  • IP address, browser type, and device information
  • Pages visited, time on site, and referral source
  • Cookie data (see our Cookie Policy)

We do not collect payment card data directly. All payments are processed by our PCI-DSS-compliant payment provider.

3. How we use your data

We process your personal data on the following lawful bases under UK GDPR:

  • Contract performance — to arrange, deliver, and communicate about your appointment and test results.
  • Explicit consent — to process Special Category health data for the provision of diagnostic services.
  • Legal obligation — to comply with our CQC registration requirements and applicable health regulations.
  • Legitimate interests — to improve our services, respond to complaints, and prevent fraud.

We will never sell your personal data to third parties, and we will never use your health data for insurance or employment purposes.

4. How long we keep your data

Medical and health records, including your test results and health report, are retained for a minimum of 8 years from the date of your appointment, or until your 25th birthday if you were under 18 at the time (though this service is restricted to adults aged 18 and over). This retention period is consistent with NHS and clinical record-keeping guidelines.

Marketing preferences and general contact data are retained for up to 3 years from your last interaction with us, unless you request deletion earlier.

5. Who we share data with

We share your personal data only with:

  • Our UKAS-accredited laboratory partners, for the purpose of sample analysis and reporting
  • GMC-registered GPs providing the included consultation
  • Our secure results portal provider (data processed under a Data Processing Agreement)
  • Payment processing providers (limited to transaction data only)
  • Regulatory bodies (ICO, CQC) where legally required

All third-party processors are bound by Data Processing Agreements and are required to maintain appropriate technical and organisational security measures.

6. Data security

We implement appropriate technical and organisational measures to protect your personal data, including:

  • TLS encryption for all data in transit
  • AES-256 encryption for health records at rest
  • Access controls limiting data access to authorised personnel only
  • Regular staff training on data protection and information security
  • Regular penetration testing and security audits
Data breach notification

In the event of a data breach that is likely to result in a risk to your rights and freedoms, we will notify you and the ICO within 72 hours of becoming aware of the breach, in accordance with UK GDPR Article 34.

7. Your rights under UK GDPR

You have the following rights regarding your personal data:

  • Right of access — to request a copy of the personal data we hold about you (Subject Access Request)
  • Right to rectification — to request correction of inaccurate or incomplete data
  • Right to erasure — to request deletion of your data where there is no compelling reason for its continued processing
  • Right to restriction — to request that we limit how we use your data
  • Right to data portability — to receive your data in a structured, machine-readable format
  • Right to object — to object to processing based on legitimate interests
  • Right to withdraw consent — where processing is based on consent, you may withdraw it at any time

Please note that certain rights may be limited where we have a legal obligation to retain clinical records. To exercise any of these rights, please contact us using the details below.

8. Cookies

Our website uses essential cookies to function correctly and, where you have given consent, analytical cookies to help us understand how visitors use the site. Please see our Cookie Policy for full details and to manage your preferences.

9. Transfers outside the UK

We do not transfer your personal data outside the UK. All laboratory processing and data storage takes place within the United Kingdom.

10. Children's data

Our services are only available to individuals aged 18 and over. We do not knowingly collect personal data from children. If you believe we have inadvertently collected data from a person under 18, please contact us immediately.

11. Changes to this policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time. The current version will always be published on this page, with the "Last updated" date shown at the top. If we make material changes, we will notify existing customers by email.
